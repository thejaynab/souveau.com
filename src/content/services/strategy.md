## Thinking Before Building

Teams often jump into development without a clear UX direction — then wonder why users churn, features go unused, and redesigns keep happening. Great UX strategy reduces churn, increases conversions, and aligns your team by clarifying the core experience your product should deliver — mapping the journey, identifying where users get stuck, and guiding them toward value.

### What You Get:
* **Requirements Mapping:** Translating high-level goals into flows and requirements your dev team can run with.
* **User Pain Points Analysis:** Identifying friction points and opportunities through real data.
* **Information Architecture:** Organizing content and navigation for intuitive user workflows.
* **Prioritized Roadmap:** Focusing on the improvements that will actually move the needle.

### The Result:
Instead of guessing what to build next, your team builds with confidence — backed by real insight. No more arguing over opinions. Just clear direction based on what users actually need.
